Place in Calamba

Banga

The Spanish name Calamba City, derived from the name of the enormous clay pot, Kalambanga, is derived from the story of a young girl who mistakenly guessed the location as Kalambanga due to her inability to speak Spanish. The name Kalan and Banga, which translates to "clay stove" and "clay pot," was mistakenly given to the Spanish soldiers, leading to the abbreviation of Calamba. The name Kalambanga is a testament to the city's rich history and cultural heritage.
4.5 (213 Reviews)

Transportation

The most convenient way to reach Banga is to take a tricycle or jeepney from the Calamba City terminal. These are readily available and will take you to Banga in a short time.

St. John the Baptist Parish Church

Catholic church in Calamba, Laguna, is St. John the Baptist Parish Church, sometimes referred to as Calamba Church. This church, which was located across from Calambanga and the Rizal Shrine, is where Jose Rizal was baptized.
4.7 (427 Reviews)

Transportation

The most convenient way to reach St. John the Baptist Parish Church is by taking public transportation such as tricycles or jeepneys available from various points in Calamba City.

Laguna de Bay

Calamba City situated along Laguna de Bay takes advantage of its opportunities. All year round, tourists are attracted to the famous Wonder Island, set in the middle of the lake. This provides not only picturesque view but more so private jet-ski facilities and boat sailing thrills. Laguna de Bay is a large shallow freshwater body in the heart of Luzon Island with an aggregate area of about 911 km2 (352 sq mi) and a shoreline of 220 km (140 mi). It is considered to be the third largest inland body of water in Southeast Asia after Tonle Sap in Cambodia and Lake Toba in Sumatra, Indonesia. Laguna de Bay is bordered by the province of Laguna in the east, west and southwest, the province of Rizal in the north to northeast, and Metropolitan Manila in the northwest. The lake has an average depth of 2.8 meters (9 ft 2 in) and its excess water is discharged through the Pasig River.
4.7 (200 Reviews)

Transportation

To visit Laguna de Bay, you can take a scenic boat ride from various points around the lake or drive along the shoreline to enjoy the view.

Rockpoint

The mysterious granite cinder block of Rockpoint looks like an ancient spearhead from the hills in the resort town of Pansol. The locals say that no amount of digging or hammering over the years could demolish this pointed rock.
4.6 (180 Reviews)

Transportation

To reach Rockpoint, you can take a jeepney or tricycle from the town center of Pansol. It's a short and scenic ride to this unique natural landmark.

Petty John Studio

Pottery making is an important source of livelihood in Laguna. John Lorenzo Pettyjohn and wife Tessy belong to an emerging generation of artist-potters who are reviving the traditional methods of pottery making, fusing them with contemporary styles of Asian and Western ceramics. From mostly functional gas-fired stoneware and porcelain, the Petty John are now experimenting with wood-and-salt firing. Located at 37 Purok 5 Bucal, Calamba City, Laguna.
4.9 (220 Reviews)

Transportation

To visit Petty John Studio, you can take a tricycle or jeepney from the city center of Calamba. The studio is located in a scenic area, providing a relaxing environment for pottery enthusiasts.

Mt. Makiling

Mt. Makiling Forest Reserve serves the interest of adventurous people who love mountain climbing, bird watching, nature gazing, and camping. Mt, Makiling with its foot resting on the side of the city is the best venue for such gratification. And for those plant lovers, ornamental gardens are abounding in Barangays Real, Pansol, and Bagong Kalsada. They sell different kinds of plants, compost, fertilizers, pots, tools, and landscaping products. Mount Makiling is an inactive stratovolcano located in the provinces of Laguna and Batangas on the island of Luzon in the Philippines. The mountain rises to an elevation of 1,090 meters above mean sea level and is the highest feature of the Laguna Volcanic Field. The volcano has no recorded historic eruption, but volcanism is still evident through geothermal features like mud springs and hot springs. South of the mountain is the Makiling–Banahaw Geothermal Plant. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology classifies the volcano as "Inactive". Mount Makiling is a state-owned forest reserve administered by the University of the Philippines Los Baños. Prior to its transfer to the university, the mountain was the first national park of the Philippines. Mount Makiling National Park was established on February 23, 1933, by Proclamation No. 552.
4.7 (190 Reviews)

Transportation

To explore Mt. Makiling, you can take a scenic drive or hike to the base of the mountain from various points in Calamba City. Guided tours are also available for those interested in learning more about the mountain's ecology and history.
